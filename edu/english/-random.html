Discursive Texts

Discursive Writing

  • - A neutral tone and formal style.
  • - Structured paragraphs that present contrasting ideas or perspectives.
  • - Logical reasoning supported by examples, evidence, or anecdotes.
  • - A conclusion that reflects on the topic without necessarily taking a strong stance.

Discursive Text Checklist

  • - Do you have an interesting topic sentence
  • - Do you have a statement that outlines multiple perspectives
  • - Is the tone neutral and formal
  • - Do you have evidence or examples to support it?
  • - Do you have a contrasting viewpoint?
  • - Do you have evidence or examples to support this perspective?
  • - Do you use linking words to show connections between viewpoints?
  • - Do you have a summary of key arguments?
  • - Do you reflect on the topic without taking a strong stance?
  • - Do you have clear sentences and proper grammar?
  • - Is your writing logically structured and easy to follow?

Conflicts

internal vs external conflicts

Internal conflict refers to a person's struggle with their own emotions or decisions, while external conflict involves a person facing challenges from outside forces, such as other people or society.

Examples

Internal conflict: I'm feeling overwhelmed by the workload

External conflict: The world is facing a global pandemic
