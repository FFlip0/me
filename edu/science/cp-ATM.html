atoms

(this includes content from physics and chemistry.)

Atoms include 3 types of subatomic particles that make up an atom:

Neutron (neutral)
Proton (positive +1)
Electron (negative −1)

Neutrons are neutral meaning they have no charge, they are in the nucleus of the atom.
Protons are positive meaning they have a positive charge (+1), they are in the nucleus of the atom.
Electrons are negative meaning they have a negative charge (−1), they are in the electron shell of the atom.

properties of matter

mass

The amount of matter in an object; it is a measure of the quantity of atoms and molecules within a substance.

volume

The amount of space that matter occupies; it can be measured in cubic units.

density

The mass of an object per unit volume; it describes how tightly packed the matter is within a given space.

weight

The force exerted on an object due to gravity; weight depends on mass but also on the acceleration due to gravity.

physical state

The state in which matter exists—solid, liquid, or gas—depending on temperature and pressure conditions.

melting point

The temperature at which a solid turns into a liquid.

boiling point

The temperature at which a liquid turns into a gas.

solubility

The ability of a substance to dissolve in another substance, typically in reference to a solid dissolving in a liquid.

conductivity

The ability of a substance to conduct heat or electricity.

malleability

The ability of a material to withstand deformation under compressive stress, such as being hammered or rolled into thin sheets.

ductility

The ability of a material to undergo significant plastic deformation before rupture or breakage, often characterised by its ability to be stretched into a wire.

Two or more atoms chemically bonded, these elements have to be diffrient not the same.
(Compounds are a part of molecules)

types of atoms

normal

These are the normal atoms that are found on the periodic table (these are also called 'elements'). It inculdes the correct ammount of protons, neutrons and electrons.

isotopes

“Different forms of the same element.” A isotope is when a element has a different number of neutrons (of the element) but the element remains the same. Isotopes are considered the same element just as a ‘isotope’ of the element, it only changes the mass number (A) of neutrons and stability.
An element's relative atomic mass is calculated by taking the average mass of all the element's isotopes.

ions

Every element on the periodic table have an equalling charge meaning they cancel out but when the number of protons or elections is changed, it becomes an ion. An atom can have a positive or negative charge (net charge) by which has more power (protons or electrons).
Negatively charged ion - Anion (-ide)
Positively charged ion - cation
