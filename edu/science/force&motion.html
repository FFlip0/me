definitions

vector quantity

Magnitude & Direction
E.G: 500m, North

scalar quantity

Value
E.G 40kg

magnitude

Value or defined (quantity which you may need to find). ( usually numbers)

mass

The matter which is the same on whatever gravity force exists on the planet (or space).

weight

The force pulled from the gravity of the planet you are on. This may also change from which planet you are on because the gravity is different…

force

Always force us either pull or push and it is measured by a value called “N” which stands for Newton.
F = ma
m is mass, a is acceleration

motion

If it is not being pushed or pulled by a force it is at rest but if it is being used then it is NOT at rest.

speed

The measurement of a object and how fast it is going from point A to B speed=d/t d= distance, t = time
E.g. 50km/h

velocity

D/T (d = displacement, T = Time)
E.g. 50km/h in the east direction.

displacement

(Distance with direction) From point A to B but instead of taking left and right you are not using left and right.

acceleration

When something is at rest or at a constant speed and it goes to a higher rate of speed which is called 'Acceleration.' (as well as time is important tosee how much time it took)

u = initial velocity = 5m/s

a = v-u/t

a = 10-5/5 = 5m/s2

Newton's laws

Newton's First Law

“An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion with the same speed & same direction… UNLESS acted upon by an UNBALANCED force”
