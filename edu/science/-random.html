dictionary

accuracy

The accuracy of an experiment is when the trend (increase/decrease/stable) of the numbers in the experiment follows the correct pattern.

precision

The precision of an experiment refers to how consistent or repeatable measurements are, regardless of whether they are close to the true value.

validity

The validity of an experiment refers to whether a measurement, experiment, or study actually measures what it claims to measure.

variables

independent variable

The variable that is altered during a scientific experiment (changes).


dependent variable

The variable being tested or measured during a scientific experiment.(Measured)


controlled variable

The variables that are kept the same during a scientific experiment or/and the variables that are required to be the same to repeat the expirement.

experiment scaffold

this is an extremely basic scaffold because I'm lazy.

1. Inquiry Question

Does the amount of water given to a plant affect its growth height?

2. Hypothesis

If a plant is given more water, then it will grow taller.

3. Variables

Independent Variable

Amount of water given to the plant.

Dependent Variable

Height of the plant.

Controlled Variable(s)
  • Type of plant
  • Type of soil
  • Amount of sunlight
  • Size of pot
  • Temperature

4. Experiment

A controlled experiment will be conducted to test the effect of water on plant growth.

5. Risk Assessment

  • Minor risk of water spillage.
  • Thorns on some plants may cause minor scratches.

6. Method

1. Obtain three identical plants of the same species.
2. Plant each in identical pots with the same type of soil.
3. Place the pots in a location with consistent sunlight.
4. Water Plant A with 50ml of water daily.
5. Water Plant B with 100ml of water daily.
6. Water Plant C with 150ml of water daily.
7. Measure the height of each plant daily for two weeks.
8. Record the data in a table.
